Using IoT to Solve Problems

What is IoT?

The term "Internet of Things" (IoT) describes an enormous number of devices that gather and share data. Numerous people have been effectively connected through the Internet, but it is now time to connect actual, ordinary objects as well. Anything may be made into a linked IoT device thanks to today's modern technologies
Imagine this: You're a patient who is at risk, and thanks to modern technology, you have access to a medical bracelet that keeps track of your vital signs like heart rate and blood pressure. Your healthcare providers would be able to access the data that this bracelet would collect as well. Your doctor might immediately access information on your vital signs if they are low and make a diagnosis without you ever leaving your bed. Depending on how bad the situation is, your doctor might immediately dispatch an ambulance to take you to the hospital. As a result, situations when medical personnel arrive on the scene too late are avoided, as are visits to the clinic on a weekly or monthly basis. This is one of the ways IoT could be so useful to billions of people. .
